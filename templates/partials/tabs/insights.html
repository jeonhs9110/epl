PROJECT HISTORY

The Evolution of FOBO AI

Eight iterations from a rough Poisson spreadsheet to the current Transformer + GAT + PPO + hybrid-ensemble pipeline. Each level reflects one concrete engineering decision that made the model materially better.

For the current architecture in detail, see the Home tab.

📚 Level-by-level progression
LEVEL 1 · Jan 2026
📊
Basic Stats

Pure Poisson on average goals. No machine learning — just a statistical baseline to beat.

LEVEL 2 · Jan 2026
🌲
Random Forest

First ML model. Random Forest classifier on W/D/L — better than chance, but brittle to non-linear patterns.

LEVEL 3 · Jan 2026
🧠
Deep Learning (MLP)

First neural net. An MLP focused on xG dominance vectors — captured the continuous signal trees kept bucketing.

LEVEL 4 · Feb 2026
⚡
Hybrid Ensemble

Combined the neural net with XGBoost. Different inductive biases → decorrelated errors → lower overall variance.

LEVEL 5 · Feb 2026
🎲
Monte Carlo + Calibration

Added Monte Carlo simulation + Platt scaling so reported probabilities match empirical hit rates. Essential for Kelly-based sizing.

LEVEL 6 · Feb 2026
🤖
RL Agent

Full agentic pipeline — GNN for context, PPO reinforcement learning for the decision policy, automatic value hunting.

LEVEL 7 · Feb 2026
📐
Tactical Awareness

Dixon-Coles NLL loss replaces cross-entropy. Dynamic ELO + tactical style-matching layer for home/away attack-defence interactions.

LEVEL 8 · NOW
🔥
Dynamic Triple Ensemble

Transformer + GAT + XGBoost + LightGBM with per-league LSTM look-back windows tuned by grid search. Stable leagues use 10 matches; volatile ones (Championship, Laliga2) use 3-5.

🎯 Decisions that moved the needle most
Switch to Dixon-Coles NLL

Level 3→7. Moving from cross-entropy W/D/L to Poisson with Dixon-Coles correction meant the model learned goal distributions, not just outcomes. Single biggest calibration win — BTTS and Over 2.5 became honest.

Adding the GAT

Level 5→6. The Graph Attention Network let the model reason about who a team beat. Made it suddenly obvious why a mid-table side punching above weight against strong opponents was underrated.

Separating prediction from decision

Level 5→6. Keeping the DL model focused on "what will happen" and giving the PPO agent its own Kelly-shaped reward for "should I act" cleaned up both training signals. Neither component had to compromise.

Level 8 is what you're using right now.
