PHASE I: THE INTELLIGENCE ENGINE

Building the Probability Map

STEP 1
🧠

Prediction Model

Deep Learning Core
What is Bivariate Poisson?

Unlike simple models that just guess "Win/Loss", Poisson Loss predicts the exact probability of every scoreline (0-0, 1-0, 2-1). "Bivariate" means it handles the correlation between Home and Away goals.

Why it Matters?

It gives us the granular "Shape of the Game", not just the result. This allows us to calculate Expected Goals (xG) dominance.

STEP 2
🕸️

Context Layer

GNN & Contrastive
What is a GNN?

A Graph Neural Network treats the league as a web. It learns "Transitive Strength": if Team A beats B, and B beats C, the GNN mathematically understands that A is stronger than C.

Why it Matters?

Momentum Penalty: A losing streak actively reduces the model's internal "Win Weight" for that team, preventing the model from overrating fallen giants.

STEP 3
⚡

Hybrid 1.5

XGBoost Ensemble
What is XGBoost?

It is an ensemble of Decision Trees trained using "Gradient Boosting". It iteratively builds trees to correct the errors (residuals) of the previous tree.

Why it Matters?

It acts as a "Spellchecker". It catches non-linear edge cases that the deep neural network missed, "boosting" accuracy by fixing specific blind spots.

PHASE II: THE SIMULATION ENGINE

Stress Testing Reality

STEP 4
🎲

Simulation

Monte Carlo Inference
The Method

We don't just predict once. We simulate the match 10,000 times in memory. We inject random chaos—red cards, early goals, injuries—to see how games evolve.

Why it Matters?

Football is low-scoring and high-variance. A single simulation is luck; 10,000 simulations reveal the True Probability Distribution beyond the noise.

PHASE III: THE EXECUTION ENGINE

Making the Decision

STEP 5
⚖️

Reality Check

Isotonic Calibration
What is Calibration?

Deep Learning models are often overconfident. Isotonic Regression is a mathematical mapping that realigns the model's "Confidence" with "Historical Truth".

Why it Matters?

To bet safely using math, 70% confidence MUST mean 70% win rate. Without this layer, the Kelly Criterion would calculate dangerous bet sizes.

STEP 6
🤖

The Sniper

RL Policy Agent
Reinforcement Learning

The agent is an AI trained to play the "Betting Game". Its goal isn't just to pick winners, but to maximize long-term Portfolio Growth.

The Strategy

It uses the Kelly Criterion to size bets based on Edge. If the risk is too high or the edge too small, it learns to PASS.

PHASE IV: TACTICAL AWARENESS (NEW)

Mathematical Precision & Context

STEP 7
📐

Dixon-Coles

Precision Loss Function
The Problem

Standard models underestimate low-scoring draws (0-0, 1-1). Poisson distribution assumes independence, but in football, a 0-0 half-time score often leads to a defensive second half.

The Solution

We use the Dixon-Coles Adjustment to mathematically force the model to respect the high probability of low-scoring assertions, drastically correcting draw predictions.

STEP 8
📈

Dynamic ELO

Strength of Schedule
Context is King

Scoring 3 goals against Real Madrid is worth more than scoring 3 against a relegation team. Standard stats don't know the difference.

The Fix

We calculate a running ELO Rating for every team. This "Context Multiplier" tells the neural net EXACTLY how impressive a result was.

STEP 9
⚔️

Tactical Interaction

Style Matching Layer
The Theory

Styles make fights. A high-pressing team might destroy a possession team but lose to a low-block counter-attack team.

The Implementation

We added a specific Bilinear Interaction Layer to the neural network. It explicitly models:
• Home Attack vs Away Defense
• Away Attack vs Home Defense

STEP 10
⏱️

Dynamic Context Limits

League-Specific Volatility Optimization
The Problem

Forcing a neural network to look at exactly the last 10 matches for every team causes hallucinations. In highly volatile leagues like the Championship or 2. Bundesliga, form from 10 weeks ago is irrelevant compared to momentum from the last 3 weeks.

The Solution

We run a mathematical optimizer that dynamically discovers the True Predictable Window for every league.
• Stable leagues (e.g. Serie A) use 10 matches of context.
• Volatile leagues (e.g. Laliga2) use 3 matches of context.
